North Stradbroke Island

Catch a short ferry to spend a day in the sun and sand and discover the indigenous history of "Straddie"

A Bit About 'Straddie'

‘Straddie’ is the world’s second largest sand island located 30km southeast of Brisbane. The island offers endless cultural and historical experiences to learn and discover the traditional owners of the land and their connection to the land and sea of the sub-tropical island.


Alongside the traditional adventures North Stradbroke Island offer beautiful beaches, national parks and gorges for the whole family to experience. The diverse range of activities is perfect to satisfy any visitor to the island.

What to Do?

There are many incredible activities you can do at ‘Straddie’. Go for a surf in the waters of Point Lookout, discover the local wildlife to find turtles, rays, dolphins, birds, koalas and wallabies or dive with the surrounding sea life to experience an entirely new world. No matter what you choose to do with your ‘Straddie’ itinerary you are guaranteed to have a memorable family experience.
